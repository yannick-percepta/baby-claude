# Company Brief — Meridian Data

Internal reference for onboarding. Synthetic, for the Hire-to-Onboard demo.

## Who we are

**Meridian Data** builds an enterprise data platform that makes every team's data
trustworthy, governed, and fast — one place to ingest, model, and serve data across
clouds. We sell to large regulated enterprises (manufacturing, financial services,
life sciences) and compete with Databricks, Snowflake, and Microsoft Fabric.

**Mission (one line):** Make every team's data something they can actually trust.

## Our values

1. **Earn trust, then keep it.** It's the product *and* how we work.
2. **Show the work.** Decisions are written down and shared, not held in heads.
3. **Bias to the customer's reality.** We design for the messy data people actually have.
4. **Move fast, stay kind.** Speed and decency are not a trade-off.
5. **Craft counts.** Details are the difference between "fine" and "trusted."

## The Design Systems team (Maya's team)

The Design Systems team owns Meridian's shared component library ("Loom"), the
design-token pipeline, and the company-wide accessibility standard. The team is the
connective tissue between Design and Engineering: every product team builds on Loom,
so the bar the Design Systems team sets becomes the bar for the whole product.
Current focus: shipping Loom v3 (new theming engine) and getting the product to
WCAG 2.2 AA across the board.

## First week at a glance (standard template)

**Day 1 (Monday)**
- 9:30 CT — Laptop & accounts check-in with IT (15 min)
- 10:00 CT — Welcome coffee with your manager
- 11:00 CT — "Meridian 101" cohort session (mission, product, how we work)
- Afternoon — Set up tools, read the team's working-agreement doc, no meetings
- 4:00 CT — Optional: end-of-day "hellos" drop-in on the team call

**Rest of week 1**
- Tue — 1:1 with your manager; meet your onboarding buddy
- Wed — Team standup + architecture/design walkthrough of your area
- Thu — Coffee chats with 2–3 cross-functional partners
- Fri — First small starter task scoped with your buddy; week-1 retro with manager

We deliberately keep week 1 light. Ramp is measured in weeks, not days.
